Global Revision

Revising my multigenre persuasive campaign

For my global revision, I returned to the multigenre campaign about cybersecurity in infrastructure. My instructor’s feedback showed that the campaign had a clear position, but it also needed more specificity, clearer audience distinctions, and stronger explanation of why the rhetorical choices would matter in professional practice. I revised each genre with that feedback in mind.

What I revised

  • Made the calls to action more concrete instead of relying on broad phrases.
  • Connected risks to actual design and planning decisions.
  • Clarified the difference between civil engineers and project planners as audiences.
  • Reworked the rhetorical analysis so it explains why the choices work, not just what they are.

PSA flyer

Original wording

Audit systems, strengthen digital security, train your staff, protect your infrastructure.

Revised wording

Audit system vulnerabilities regularly, build cybersecurity checks into planning and design reviews, train staff to recognize threats, and protect infrastructure before a problem becomes a public safety issue.

Why this revision mattered The original line had urgency, but it stayed generic. The revision keeps the same message while adding language that sounds more connected to actual engineering practice. It also pushes the audience toward action earlier in the process instead of after the system is already in place.

Infographic

Original wording

Cyberattacks can cause system shutdowns, economic damage, and safety risks.

Revised wording

Cyberattacks can cause system shutdowns, economic damage, and safety risks, especially when digital systems are treated as an afterthought during infrastructure planning. These risks become more manageable when engineers and planners treat cybersecurity as part of system design rather than a separate IT concern.

Why this revision mattered My teacher wanted a clearer link between the dangers I named and the decisions professionals would actually make. This revision does that. It moves the infographic away from just describing consequences and toward explaining where those consequences come from.

Tri-fold brochure

Original wording

Cybersecurity must be treated like any other design requirement.

Revised wording

Cybersecurity must be treated like any other design requirement by being built into the project from the earliest planning stages, not added later patched on after the infrastructure is already operating.

Why this revision mattered This change makes the argument more precise. Instead of stating a principle in a general way, it explains when cybersecurity should enter the process and why waiting until later weakens the system.

Rhetorical analysis

Original wording

The flyer is eye catching, the infographic gives a great baseline of the topic, and the brochure is an invitation to action.

Revised wording

The flyer works because its "Public Safety Alert" framing creates urgency in a way that fits how engineers and project managers already think about risk. The infographic then slows the message down and gives that urgency a factual base, while the brochure extends the argument into a more professional and instructional form. Together, the genres do not just repeat the same point. They move the audience from awareness to consequence to action.

Why this revision mattered This was the biggest change. In the original draft, I mostly described what each genre was doing. In the revision, I focused more on why those choices would matter for the intended audience and how the campaign works as a sequence instead of three separate items.

What this revision demonstrates about my writing now

This revision shows that I am better at recognizing when a draft has the right idea but not yet the right level of precision. More importantly, it shows that I can revise for purpose, audience, and rhetorical effect instead of only revising for wording. I still kept the original campaign’s core argument, but I made it more specific, more useful, and more aware of how a professional audience would respond.
