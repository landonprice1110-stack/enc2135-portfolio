ENC2135 Project 3 ePortfolio

This portfolio shows how my writing became more focused, more purposeful, and much more aware of audience.

At the beginning of the semester, I was mostly trying to get my ideas onto the page and explain them clearly. By the end, I was thinking more carefully about what each piece of writing needed to do, who it was speaking to, and how revision could make the argument stronger instead of just cleaner.

What this portfolio is arguing

This site is not just a collection of assignments. It is meant to show a pattern in my work across the semester. Looking at the pages together, the main change is that I moved from broad, general writing toward writing that is more deliberate in its structure, more specific in its claims, and more aware of what a particular audience actually needs.

Development in focus

My earlier work shows that I could gather ideas, but I was still learning how to narrow a topic and build a sharper claim. Later work is more focused and less dependent on broad explanation.

Development in audience awareness

The multigenre campaign pushed me to think beyond a classroom reader. I had to consider how civil engineers and project planners would react to different formats, levels of detail, and types of evidence.

Development through revision

The global revision page shows the clearest change. I used feedback to make my recommendations more concrete, explain my rhetorical choices more fully, and connect risks to real decisions.

Development through reflection

The reflection pulls everything together by explaining what changed in my writing process and what I will carry into future writing situations.

How to read this portfolio

Start with the revision

The revised project is the strongest evidence of growth because it shows exactly what I changed and why those changes mattered.

Use the artifacts as context

The artifact page shows where my writing was earlier in the process and how outside feedback helped shape later decisions.

End with the reflection

The reflection explains the larger pattern across the semester instead of treating each assignment as a separate piece of work.
